Compound Interest


Compound Interest.

Compound interest (or compounding interest) is interest calculated on the initial principal, which also includes all of the accumulated interest from previous periods on a deposit or loan.


Example:

John has a savings acount with $1000, every year he recieves 10% Compound interest, how much money is in his acount after 2 years?


Example Explination:

The formula to work out compound interest is A = P(1 + r)^(t)

in this case a is the final result so we will leave it last, P is the initial

investment in our case 1000, r is the anual interest rate in our case 10% but it

has to be in decimal so 10% = 0.1, finally t is the time its been there for us 2 years

(A = P(1 + r)^(nt)) = (A = 1000(1 + 0.1)^(2)) = 1210


Try it yourself Question:

Jane has an acount with compound interest of 25%, currently she has $500, how much will Jane have after 15 years?
